Dataset Description

The Respiratory Sound Database was developed specifically to support the research challenge at the International Conference on Biomedical Health Informatics - ICBHI 2017. It includes a total of 920 audio samples that have been annotated and come from 126 patients, providing a combined recording time of 5.5 hours and containing 6898 respiratory cycles. The cycles were assessed by experts who determined if they had crackles, wheezes, both, or no atypical respiratory sounds. Of the 6898 cycles, 1864 contained wheezes, 886 contained crackles, and 506 contained both. The recordings varied in duration, lasting from 10 to 90 seconds, and were made using various equipment pieces. The database also includes the locations on the chest where the recordings were taken. Some respiratory cycles have high noise levels, creating a realistic representation of real-world scenarios.
