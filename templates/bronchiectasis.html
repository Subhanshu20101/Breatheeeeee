Probable Causes: - Repeated lung infections, such as pneumonia or tuberculosis, can cause damage to the bronchi, leading to bronchiectasis. - Inherited disorders, such as cystic fibrosis, can also cause bronchiectasis. - In rare cases, bronchiectasis may be caused by an autoimmune disease or a problem with the immune system.

Prevention: - Quitting smoking and avoiding exposure to secondhand smoke can help prevent bronchiectasis. - It is also important to treat lung infections promptly to reduce the risk of developing bronchiectasis.
