Probable Causes: - Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) is the most common cause of bronchiolitis, particularly in infants and young children. - Other viruses, such as rhinovirus, adenovirus, and influenza virus, can also cause bronchiolitis. - Exposure to tobacco smoke, air pollution, or other irritants can increase the risk of developing bronchiolitis.

Prevention: - Frequent hand washing and avoiding close contact with people who have respiratory infections can help prevent the spread of the viruses that cause bronchiolitis. - Avoiding exposure to secondhand smoke, air pollution, and other irritants can also help reduce the risk of developing bronchiolitis.
